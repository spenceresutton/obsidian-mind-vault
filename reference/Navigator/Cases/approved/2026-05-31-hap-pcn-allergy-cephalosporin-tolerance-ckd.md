# PNA Case 16: HAP with Penicillin Allergy and Renal Dysfunction

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer Sutton

## Prompt

SS is a 71-year-old male with a past medical history significant for CKD stage 3b (baseline creatinine 1.8 mg/dL, baseline CrCl approximately 42 mL/min), type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and benign prostatic hyperplasia who was admitted seven days ago for an elective total hip arthroplasty. He received cefazolin 2g IV for surgical prophylaxis without adverse reaction. His surgical admission represents his first hospitalization in over a year and he has not required antibiotic therapy in the preceding 90 days. His respiratory culture history on file is unremarkable. Notably, the patient has a documented allergy to penicillin (hives — 20-year history). On chart review, he has received therapy with cephalexin, ceftriaxone, and cefpodoxime without incident.

On hospital day 7, he develops new fever, productive cough with purulent sputum, and worsening dyspnea requiring supplemental oxygen. A chest X-ray is obtained and compared to the pre-operative film. Blood cultures and sputum culture are sent. MRSA nasal PCR is obtained. His allergy history is reviewed prior to antibiotic selection.

He appears acutely ill with mild to moderate respiratory distress. Lung exam reveals decreased breath sounds at the right lower lobe with dullness to percussion and fine crackles at the right base. He is alert and oriented to person, place, and time.

He takes lisinopril 10 mg daily (held in the setting of AKI), metoprolol succinate 50 mg daily, atorvastatin 40 mg nightly, metformin 1000 mg BID (held per perioperative protocol), aspirin 81 mg daily, and tamsulosin 0.4 mg daily.
