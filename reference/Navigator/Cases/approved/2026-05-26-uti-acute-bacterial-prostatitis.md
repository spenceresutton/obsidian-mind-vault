# UTI Case 8: Acute bacterial prostatitis

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

SS is an 87-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, type 2 diabetes mellitus, asthma, and COPD who presents to the emergency department with a 3-day history of fever, perineal pain, dysuria, and progressive difficulty urinating. He reports subjective chills and malaise beginning 3 days prior. On the day of presentation he noted a near-complete inability to void, prompting the ED visit.

On arrival, vitals are: T 38.6°C, HR 102, BP 148/84, RR 18, SpO2 95% on 2L NC (baseline SpO2 94% on room air per prior records). Digital rectal exam reveals a markedly tender, warm, and boggy prostate. Bladder scan on arrival demonstrates 680 mL of retained urine. A urethral catheter is placed with immediate relief of urinary retention and return of cloudy urine. Urinalysis and urine culture are obtained from the catheter. Blood cultures are collected. The patient is admitted to general medicine and empiric ceftriaxone 2g IV q24h is initiated.

It is now hospital day 2. Blood cultures are finalized with no growth to date. The urine culture has finalized with full susceptibilities. The patient has defervesced and his vital signs have normalized: T 37.2°C, HR 84, BP 136/78. He remains on the urethral catheter for ongoing urinary retention management, with urology consulted for BPH-related outflow obstruction. He is tolerating a regular diet.

## Imaging

**CT Abdomen/Pelvis with Contrast (obtained on admission):**
1. Marked prostatic enlargement with heterogeneous enhancement and surrounding fat stranding, consistent with acute prostatitis. No drainable prostatic abscess identified.
2. Urinary bladder markedly distended with wall thickening consistent with chronic outlet obstruction. Foley catheter in place on post-catheter scout image.
3. Bilateral kidneys normal in size and enhancement. No hydronephrosis, nephrolithiasis, or perinephric stranding bilaterally.
4. No pelvic free fluid, pelvic lymphadenopathy, or alternative source of infection identified.

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Acute bacterial prostatitis
  - Recognize Citrobacter Koseri, unlike some other citrobacter, does not harbor an inducible AmpC gene
  - Recognize continuation of ceftriaxone as appropriate while in the hospital, however advocate for oral conversion
  - Recognize that bacterial penetration plays a role in agent selection
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Recognize continuation of ceftriaxone as appropriate while in the hospital, however advocate for oral conversion
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):*
  - TMP/SMX 2 DS tablets BID (or 1.5 DS tablets TID) for 2-6 weeks
