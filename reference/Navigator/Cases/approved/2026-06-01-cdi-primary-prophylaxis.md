# CDI Case 11: Primary Prophylaxis Candidate

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

EV is a 52-year-old female with a past medical history significant for moderate-to-severe ulcerative colitis, hypertension, and iron deficiency anemia who is admitted with 3 days of productive cough, fever, and progressive dyspnea. She was diagnosed with a UC flare 2 weeks ago and was started on prednisone 40 mg PO daily at that time. She also receives infliximab 5 mg/kg IV every 8 weeks for maintenance therapy, with her most recent infusion 3 weeks ago. A chest X-ray confirms right lower lobe pneumonia and the team plans to initiate antibiotics. She has no prior history of CDI. On arrival, vitals are: T 38.8°C, HR 104, BP 118/72, RR 22, SpO₂ 92% on room air, improving to 96% on 2L NC. She appears mildly ill. Lung exam reveals decreased breath sounds and dullness to percussion at the right base. She takes prednisone, infliximab (infusion), mesalamine, ferrous sulfate, and lisinopril. Her allergy history is reviewed prior to antibiotic selection.

**Ht:** 5'5"   **Wt:** 68 kg   **BMI:** 25.4   **Baseline SCr:** 0.7 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA
