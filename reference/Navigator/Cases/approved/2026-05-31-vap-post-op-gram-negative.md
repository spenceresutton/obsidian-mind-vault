# PNA Case 17: VAP

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer Sutton

## Prompt

SS is a 68-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and benign prostatic hyperplasia who underwent an elective left hemicolectomy for sigmoid colon adenocarcinoma four days ago. He received cefazolin 2g IV as a single pre-operative prophylactic dose and remains intubated and mechanically ventilated post-operatively due to prolonged ventilator dependence. His surgical admission is his first hospitalization in over a year.

On post-operative day 4, nursing documents new fever, increased purulent secretions on endotracheal suctioning, and a slight increase in FiO2 requirement from 0.35 to 0.45 to maintain SpO2. Blood cultures and an endotracheal aspirate are sent. A portable chest X-ray is obtained and compared to the post-operative day 1 film. MRSA nasal PCR is sent per VAP protocol.

He remains on mechanical ventilation (SIMV mode). Lung exam reveals coarse breath sounds bilaterally with decreased aeration at the left lower lobe. Increased purulent secretions are present on endotracheal suctioning. He is sedated on a propofol infusion and does not respond to verbal stimuli.

He takes lisinopril 10 mg daily, metformin 1000 mg BID (held since admission per perioperative protocol), amlodipine 5 mg daily, and tamsulosin 0.4 mg daily. He has no documented drug allergies.

ICU vitals (post-operative day 4): T 38.8°C, HR 104, BP 118/72 (no vasopressors), Vent: SIMV RR 16 set/22 total, FiO2 0.45, PEEP 5 cmH2O, SpO2 97%.

Ht: 5'10"   Wt: 86 kg   BMI: 27.6   Baseline SCr: 0.9 mg/dL   Allergies: NKDA
