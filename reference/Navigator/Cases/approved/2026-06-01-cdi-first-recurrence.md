# CDI Case 6: First Recurrence

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

CL is a 66-year-old female with a past medical history significant for hypertension and osteoporosis who presents with 3 days of recurrent watery diarrhea with 5–7 loose stools per day and crampy lower abdominal pain. She was diagnosed with CDI approximately 5 weeks ago following a course of amoxicillin-clavulanate and completed a full 10-day course of vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H. Her symptoms resolved within a week of completing treatment. She had been doing well until 3 days ago when diarrhea returned. She denies fever, hematochezia, or vomiting. She has no new antibiotic exposures since her initial CDI treatment. On arrival, vitals are: T 37.6°C, HR 84, BP 138/80, RR 16, SpO₂ 97% on room air. Abdomen is soft with mild diffuse tenderness and active bowel sounds; no guarding or distension. She takes lisinopril, alendronate, calcium carbonate, and vitamin D. She has no known drug allergies.
