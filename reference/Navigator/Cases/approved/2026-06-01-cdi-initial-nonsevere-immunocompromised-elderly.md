# CDI Case 10: Initial Nonsevere CDI, Moderately Immunocompromised Elderly Patient

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

RF is a 73-year-old female with a past medical history significant for rheumatoid arthritis on methotrexate and low-dose corticosteroids, hypertension, and osteopenia who presents with 5 days of watery diarrhea with 5–6 loose stools per day and mild lower abdominal cramping. She completed a 7-day course of cephalexin 2 weeks ago for a skin and soft tissue infection. She has no prior history of CDI. On arrival, vitals are: T 37.5°C, HR 82, BP 132/78, RR 16, SpO₂ 97% on room air. Abdomen is soft with mild diffuse tenderness, active bowel sounds, and no guarding or distension. She takes methotrexate 15 mg PO weekly, prednisone 10 mg PO daily, hydroxychloroquine 200 mg PO BID, folic acid, lisinopril, and calcium carbonate with vitamin D. She has no known drug allergies.
