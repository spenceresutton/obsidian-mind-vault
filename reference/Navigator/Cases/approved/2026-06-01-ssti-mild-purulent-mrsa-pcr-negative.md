# SSTI Case 8: Purulent SSTI — MRSA Nasal PCR Negative

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

SS is a 44-year-old female with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department with a 3-day history of a painful, enlarging lump on the right thigh. The area has been progressively worsening and is now draining a small amount of cloudy fluid. She denies fever or chills. She has no prior MRSA infections, no recent hospitalizations, and no penetrating trauma.

On presentation, vitals are: T 37.2°C, HR 82, BP 124/78, RR 14, SpO2 99% on room air. Exam reveals a 3 cm fluctuant abscess on the anterior right thigh with 1–2 cm of surrounding erythema. A small amount of purulent material is visible at the surface. No crepitus. No lymphangitic streaking. No regional lymphadenopathy.

Incision and drainage is performed. Approximately 6 mL of thick, purulent material is expressed. The wound is loosely packed.

**Ht:** 5'6"   **Wt:** 72 kg   **BMI:** 26.1   **Baseline SCr:** 0.8 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Culture Results

MRSA Nasal PCR: Negative

MRSA Inguinal PCR: Negative
