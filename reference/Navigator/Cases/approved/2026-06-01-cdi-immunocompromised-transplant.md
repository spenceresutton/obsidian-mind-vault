# CDI Case 8: CDI in Immunocompromised Patient (Transplant)

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

GN is a 58-year-old male with a past medical history significant for ESRD status post deceased donor kidney transplant 14 months ago, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes mellitus who presents with 5 days of watery diarrhea with 6–8 loose stools per day and diffuse abdominal cramping. He completed a 7-day course of ciprofloxacin 3 weeks ago for a urinary tract infection. He has no prior history of CDI. He is on maintenance immunosuppression with tacrolimus 2 mg PO BID, mycophenolate mofetil 500 mg PO BID, and prednisone 5 mg PO daily. He denies fever, hematochezia, or nausea. On arrival, vitals are: T 37.9°C, HR 90, BP 134/82, RR 16, SpO₂ 97% on room air. Abdomen is soft with mild diffuse tenderness, active bowel sounds, and no guarding or distension. He also takes amlodipine, insulin glargine, and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole 80/400 mg PO daily (Pneumocystis prophylaxis — reinstated by the transplant team at last visit). He has no known drug allergies.

**Ht:** 5'8"   **Wt:** 80 kg   **BMI:** 27.0   **Baseline SCr:** 1.2–1.4 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Initial Nonsevere CDI in a kidney transplant patient on triple immunosuppression
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Fidaxomicin 200 mg PO BID × 10 days — **strongly preferred** over vancomycin in immunocompromised patients given elevated recurrence risk; **Infectious Disease approval required at SJN prior to use**
  - Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H × 10 days — acceptable if fidaxomicin is not approved
  - Infectious Disease consult recommended given transplant context and recurrence risk
  - Discuss with transplant team — immunosuppression cannot be discontinued; assess whether any agents can be temporarily minimized in coordination with transplant
  - Discontinue ciprofloxacin — course is already complete; no further action needed on the inciting agent
  - Place in enteric isolation; soap-and-water hand hygiene
  - **Anti-peristaltic agents are contraindicated**
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):*
  - If symptoms have not resolved by day 10, extension of treatment duration is reasonable given immunocompromised status — decision made in coordination with ID and transplant team
  - Secondary prophylaxis: for any future systemic antibiotic courses, oral vancomycin 125 mg PO Q24H or Q12H should be strongly considered given immunosuppression as an ongoing recurrence risk factor; continue prophylaxis for 5–7 days after antibiotic completion
  - If recurrence occurs: Rebyota and Vowst have limited data in immunosuppressed patients — microbiome replacement decisions in this population require ID and transplant team guidance and should not be initiated without specialist involvement
  - Test-of-cure is NOT recommended
