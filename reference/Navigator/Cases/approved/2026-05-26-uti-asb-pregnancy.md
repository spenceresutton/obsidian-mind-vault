# UTI Case 5: Asymptomatic Bacteriuria in pregnancy

**Difficulty:** easy
**Author:** unknown

## Prompt

SS is a 29-year-old female with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department at 22 weeks gestation with a 2-day history of nausea and vomiting. She reports decreased oral intake and mild lightheadedness. She denies fever, chills, dysuria, urinary frequency, urgency, flank pain, vaginal bleeding, or decreased fetal movement. She has no abdominal pain. Her prenatal course has been uncomplicated to date. She takes prenatal vitamins daily and has no documented drug allergies.

On presentation, vitals are: T 37.0°C, HR 96, BP 108/66, RR 16, SpO2 99% on room air. She appears mildly dehydrated. Abdomen is gravid and non-tender. Fundal height is consistent with gestational age. No CVA tenderness bilaterally. Fetal heart tones are reassuring on Doppler. The ED team initiates IV fluid resuscitation for dehydration and obtains a urinalysis as part of the routine workup.

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Asymptomatic bacteriuria in pregnancy
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Nitrofurantoin 100 mg BID x5 days
  - TMP/SMX 1 DS BID x5 days or cephalexin 500 mg q6h x7 days also acceptable, but realistically if nitrofurantoin can be used it should be
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):* N/A
