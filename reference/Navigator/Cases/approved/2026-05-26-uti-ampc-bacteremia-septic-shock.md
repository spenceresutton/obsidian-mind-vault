# UTI Case 1: AmpC Producing, Gram-negative bacteremia with Septic Shock

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

SS is an 82 year old male with past medical history significant for asthma, COPD, T2DM, heart failure and stroke (paraplegia, longterm indwelling foley) who initially presented to the emergency department from his long term care facility two days ago when aids noticed decreased alertness (patient is AOx4 at baseline), increased lethargy and an episode of rigors. On arrival to the emergency department, T 36.1, HR 94, BP 83/47, RR 22, SpO2 92% on 2L NC (not on home O2). Ht 5'7", Wt 72 kg, Baseline SCr ~1.1. Given low blood pressure the patient was started on a norepinephrine gtt and provided a bolus of fluid. Bedside CXR without acute pulmonary process. The patient was admitted to the ICU. Urine, respiratory, and blood cultures were collected.

- Blood Cx 2/2 E. Cloacae, ampicillin (R), ceftriaxone (S), Cefepime (S), TMP/SMX (R), Levofloxacin (R), Meropenem (S), Piperacillin-Tazobactam (S).
- Respiratory Culture: Stenotrophomonas maltophilia, TMP/SMX (S), Levofloxacin (R), Minocycline (S)

## Imaging

**CXR:**
1. No acute pulmonary process. No focal consolidation, airspace opacity, or infiltrate to suggest pneumonia on this examination.
2. COPD/emphysema — hyperinflation with flattened hemidiaphragms and peripheral vascular attenuation, consistent with known chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.
3. Bibasilar subsegmental atelectasis, right greater than left — expected in the setting of COPD, supine positioning, and reduced respiratory excursion. No lobar collapse.
4. Mild cardiomegaly — stable in appearance, consistent with known heart failure. No radiographic evidence of acute decompensation.

**CT/AP:**
1. Right pyelonephritis — multifocal wedge-shaped areas of decreased parenchymal enhancement with perinephric fat stranding and Gerota's fascia thickening. No obstructing calculus, hydronephrosis, abscess, or gas-forming infection to suggest complicated obstructive uropathy or emphysematous pyelonephritis.
2. Catheter-associated cystitis — diffuse bladder wall thickening with Foley catheter in situ. Correlation with urinalysis and urine culture recommended.
3. Mild right ureteral wall thickening — likely inflammatory, in continuity with the right renal pelvis. No hydroureter or obstructing lesion.
4. Benign prostatic hypertrophy — incidental; may contribute to urinary stasis and susceptibility to ascending infection. Urology follow-up as clinically indicated.
5. No nephrolithiasis, abscess, perinephric collection, or pneumaturia identified.

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:**
  - Patient is in septic shock (supported by vasopressors and SOFA Score)
    - Leukopenia is concerning for poor outcomes
    - Elevated inflammatory markers (CRP, Procal)
    - Elevated Lactate
  - Source is Urinary Tract (Pyelonephritis)
    - Supported by CTAP and Urinalysis
    - Foley catheter should be replaced if not already
    - **Foul smelling urine should not be mentioned. It is language that is thrown around a lot but no data has any association with smell and infection. It would be more professional to glaze over it.**
  - Presentation is not concerning for pneumonia given no acute pulmonary process identified in CXR
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):* **Assessed after inputting the "Prompt" paragraph without any additional lab, culture or imaging findings.**
  - Piperacillin-Tazobactam 4.5g IV q8h over 4 hours x 7 days
  - Cefepime 1g q12h x7 days
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):*
  - Cefepime 1g q12h x7 days
