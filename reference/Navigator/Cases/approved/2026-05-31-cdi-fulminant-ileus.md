# CDI Case 3: Fulminant CDI with Ileus

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

RB is a 68-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, coronary artery disease, and type 2 diabetes mellitus who is transferred from the medical floor to the ICU with worsening abdominal distension, absent bowel sounds, and hemodynamic instability. He was admitted 5 days ago for healthcare-associated pneumonia and has been on piperacillin-tazobactam since admission. Over the past 24 hours he developed 8–10 watery stools, progressive abdominal pain, and distension. This morning he became hypotensive with MAP persistently below 65 mmHg despite 2L IV fluid resuscitation, and norepinephrine was initiated at 0.06 mcg/kg/min. On ICU arrival, vitals are: T 39.2°C, HR 118, BP 84/52 (MAP 63 on vasopressor), RR 24, SpO₂ 93% on 4L NC. He appears acutely ill and diaphoretic. Abdomen is markedly distended, tympanic, and diffusely tender with absent bowel sounds; no voluntary guarding, but the patient does not tolerate deep palpation. He is lethargic but arousable and oriented to person only. He takes aspirin 81 mg, metoprolol, lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin at home. Metformin was held on admission. He has no known drug allergies.

**Ht:** 5'10"   **Wt:** 91 kg   **BMI:** 29.2   **Baseline SCr:** 1.0 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Imaging

**CT Abdomen and Pelvis with Contrast**

1. Marked pancolonic wall thickening with pericolonic fat stranding — pattern consistent with severe colitis. Findings are most pronounced in the transverse and descending colon.
2. Significant colonic distension throughout. Transverse colon diameter measuring 8.4 cm — approaching toxic megacolon threshold.
3. No free intraperitoneal air identified. No evidence of colonic perforation on this study.
4. Small volume ascites in the right lower quadrant and pelvis.
5. No intra-abdominal abscess identified.
6. Impression: Pancolonic colitis with impending toxic megacolon. Findings are consistent with severe *Clostridioides difficile* colitis in the appropriate clinical context. Surgical consultation strongly recommended.

  - **INCORRECT: Oral metronidazole — not appropriate for fulminant CDI; IV metronidazole is the correct adjunct**
  - **INCORRECT: Omitting the retention enema — when ileus is present, oral/NG vancomycin may not reach the distal colon; rectal instillation is required**
