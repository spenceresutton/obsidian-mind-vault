# UTI Case 4: Acute uncomplicated cystitis

**Difficulty:** easy
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

SS is a 26-year-old female with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department with a 2-day history of dysuria, urinary frequency, and urgency. She denies fever, chills, nausea, vomiting, or flank pain. She has no costovertebral angle tenderness on exam. She is sexually active, uses barrier contraception, and her last menstrual period was 10 days ago. Urine pregnancy test is negative. She reports one prior episode of a urinary tract infection two years ago, treated with an antibiotic she cannot recall, which resolved without complication. She takes no daily medications and has no documented drug allergies.

On presentation, vitals are within normal limits: T 37.0°C, HR 76, BP 114/72, RR 15, SpO2 99% on room air. Physical exam is unremarkable. Abdomen is soft and non-tender. No suprapubic tenderness. No CVA tenderness bilaterally. A urinalysis is obtained via clean-catch midstream technique.

Given the clinical presentation and UA findings, the ED team is determining the appropriate empiric antibiotic regimen and duration prior to discharge. Urine culture is sent but results are pending. No blood cultures are collected. No imaging is obtained.

Ht: 5'5"   Wt: 64 kg   BMI: 23.6   Baseline SCr: ~0.7 mg/dL   Allergies: NKDA

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Uncomplicated cystitis
  - Culture and imaging are unnecessary given this presentation
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Nitrofurantoin 100 mg BID **(Strong preference)**
  - Return precautions
  - **Do not recommend fosfomycin or fluoroquinolones**
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):* N/A
