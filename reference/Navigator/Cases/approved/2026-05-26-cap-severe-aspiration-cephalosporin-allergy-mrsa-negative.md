# CAP Case 12: Severe Aspiration Pneumonia with Cephalosporin Anaphylaxis

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer Sutton

## Prompt

SS is a 61-year-old female with a past medical history significant for COPD (GOLD II), GERD, esophageal dysmotility, and type 2 diabetes mellitus who is admitted directly to the ICU from the emergency department with severe aspiration pneumonia. Family reports a witnessed aspiration event at home 3 days prior. Her esophageal dysmotility has been a recurrent source of aspiration risk and she has been followed by gastroenterology. She presented to the ED with worsening dyspnea and progressive hypoxia. In the ED, she received 2L of IV crystalloid for hypotension without adequate hemodynamic response. On reassessment, her MAP remained below 60 mmHg and norepinephrine was initiated. She was placed on a 40% Venturi mask with persistent hypoxia and transferred directly to the ICU for further management.

On ICU arrival, vitals are: T 39.2°C, HR 122, BP 82/52 (MAP 62 on norepinephrine 0.08 mcg/kg/min), RR 28, SpO2 92% on 40% Venturi mask. She appears acutely ill and in significant respiratory distress. Lung exam reveals decreased breath sounds at the left base with dullness to percussion and coarse crackles bilaterally. She is alert but fatigued and only partially cooperative with exam.

She takes tiotropium, an albuterol inhaler PRN, omeprazole, and metformin. Her allergy history is reviewed prior to antibiotic selection.

Ht: 5'4"   Wt: 71 kg   BMI: 26.7   Baseline SCr: 0.9 mg/dL   Allergies: Cephalexin (anaphylaxis)

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Severe aspiration community-acquired pneumonia with history of cephalosporin allergy (anaphylaxis).

- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Vancomycin IV (weight-based per pharmacy protocol, target AUC/MIC 400–600)
  - Aztreonam 2g IV q8h
  - Comment that atypical coverage is not indicated for aspiration pneumonia
  - Comment that anaerobic coverage is not indicated for aspiration pneumonia
  - **Despite a negative MRSA nasal PCR, vancomycin is required for streptococcal coverage.** Aztreonam has no gram-positive activity — it does not cover Streptococcus pneumoniae. A negative MRSA PCR removes MRSA as a concern but does not eliminate the gram-positive gap when the backbone antibiotic cannot provide that coverage.
  - Comment that treatment duration is 5 days
  - **Do not recommend fluoroquinolones without intolerance to aztreonam** (fluoroquinolone may be appropriate step-down)
