# CDI Case 7: Second Recurrence — Microbiome Replacement

**Difficulty:** hard
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

WS is a 70-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and CKD stage 3b (baseline creatinine 1.6 mg/dL, CrCl ~54 mL/min) who presents with 4 days of recurrent watery diarrhea with 6–8 loose stools per day and crampy abdominal pain. He was diagnosed with CDI 6 months ago and treated with vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H × 10 days. He had a first recurrence 3 months ago treated with fidaxomicin extended-pulsed (200 mg BID × 5 days, then every other day × 20 days). His symptoms resolved after completing the fidaxomicin course but have now returned. He has no new antibiotic exposures. He is tolerating a regular diet, able to swallow capsules without difficulty, and is hemodynamically stable. On arrival, vitals are: T 37.8°C, HR 86, BP 144/82, RR 16, SpO₂ 96% on room air. Abdomen is soft with mild diffuse tenderness, active bowel sounds, and no guarding or distension. He takes lisinopril, amlodipine, atorvastatin, and aspirin 81 mg daily. He has no known drug allergies.
