# PNA Case 15: HAP with Culture-Confirmed MRSA

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer Sutton

## Prompt

SS is a 74-year-old female with a past medical history significant for COPD (GOLD II), GERD, and hypertension who was admitted nine days ago for a COPD exacerbation. She was started on methylprednisolone 40 mg IV q12h on admission and transitioned to prednisone 40 mg PO daily after 48 hours of clinical improvement. A prior hospitalization nine months ago for a healthcare-associated pneumonia resulted in a MRSA-positive nasal swab obtained during ICU triage.

On hospital day 6, she developed new fever to 39.2°C, increased purulent sputum production, worsening dyspnea, and an oxygen requirement rising from 2L nasal cannula to 5L. Chest X-ray showed new right lower lobe consolidation. Blood cultures and sputum culture were sent and MRSA nasal PCR was obtained. Given her prior MRSA isolation within the past year, empiric piperacillin-tazobactam and vancomycin were initiated.

She is now on hospital day 8, 48 hours after culture collection, with preliminary culture results available. She appears acutely ill with moderate respiratory distress. Lung exam reveals decreased breath sounds at the right lower lobe with coarse crackles at the right base. She is alert and oriented to person and place but is confused about the date (baseline A&Ox4, currently A&Ox3).

She takes tiotropium bromide 18 mcg inhaled daily, albuterol MDI 2 puffs PRN, salmeterol/fluticasone 50/500 mcg 1 puff BID, omeprazole 20 mg daily, amlodipine 5 mg daily, and atorvastatin 40 mg nightly. She has no documented drug allergies.
