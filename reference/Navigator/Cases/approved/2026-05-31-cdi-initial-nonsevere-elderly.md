# CDI Case 1: Initial Nonsevere CDI, No Complications

**Difficulty:** easy
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

JK is a 67-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia who presents with 4 days of watery, non-bloody diarrhea with 6–8 loose stools per day and mild diffuse abdominal cramping. He completed a 10-day course of clindamycin 3 weeks ago for a dental infection. He denies fever, hematochezia, or nausea. He has no prior history of CDI and is not immunocompromised. On arrival, vitals are: T 37.8°C, HR 88, BP 136/84, RR 16, SpO₂ 97% on room air. Abdominal exam reveals mild diffuse tenderness without guarding, rebound, or peritoneal signs. He takes lisinopril, atorvastatin, and aspirin 81 mg daily. He has no known drug allergies.

**Ht:** 5'11"   **Wt:** 84 kg   **BMI:** 25.7   **Baseline SCr:** 0.8 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Initial Nonsevere CDI
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):*
  - Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H × 10 days
  - Fidaxomicin 200 mg PO BID × 10 days is the SJN-preferred agent but **requires Infectious Disease approval at SJN prior to use**
  - Age ≥65 creates a soft consideration for fidaxomicin up front given modestly elevated recurrence risk; vancomycin is reasonable first-line with a single risk factor and given fidaxomicin's cost
  - If fidaxomicin is selected: confirm ID approval is obtained prior to ordering
  - Place in enteric isolation (contact precautions); soap-and-water hand hygiene — alcohol-based hand rubs do not eliminate *C. difficile* spores
  - Assess and discontinue unnecessary systemic antibiotics
  - **Do not use IV vancomycin — does not achieve adequate luminal concentrations and is not effective for CDI**
  - **Do not use oral metronidazole — inferior to vancomycin and fidaxomicin; reserved only when no other options are available**
  - **Anti-peristaltic agents are contraindicated**
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):*
  - If symptoms have not resolved by day 10, extension is reasonable to consider in patients ≥65 years or on concomitant systemic antibiotics — extension is not the default
  - Test-of-cure is NOT recommended — PCR and toxin assays may remain positive for weeks after clinical resolution
