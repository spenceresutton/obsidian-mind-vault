# UTI Case 3: Acute Pyelonephritis without complications

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** unknown

## Prompt

SS is a 24-year-old female with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department with a 3-day history of progressive right flank pain, fever, chills, nausea, and vomiting. She reports dysuria and urinary frequency beginning approximately 5 days prior to presentation, which she initially attributed to a urinary tract infection and did not seek care for. She denies vaginal discharge. She is sexually active, uses oral contraceptives, and her last menstrual period was 2 weeks ago. She has no prior history of urinary tract infections or kidney stones. She takes no medications other than her oral contraceptive pill. On arrival to the ED, vitals are notable for T 38.9°C, HR 108, BP 106/68, RR 18, SpO2 99% on room air. She appears uncomfortable and is diffusely tender to palpation in the right costovertebral angle. Pelvic exam is deferred given low suspicion for gynecologic etiology. A urine pregnancy test is negative. UA and blood cultures are obtained, and CT abdomen/pelvis with contrast is performed given severity of presentation and concern for complicated pyelonephritis. The patient is admitted to general medicine for IV fluid resuscitation and empiric antibiotic therapy. Urine and blood cultures are pending. The primary team reviews her allergy history prior to antibiotic selection. Ht 5'4", Wt 62 kg

**Allergy:** Augmentin (Diarrhea)

## Imaging

**CT Abdomen/Pelvis with Contrast:**
1. Right pyelonephritis — multifocal wedge-shaped areas of decreased and heterogeneous parenchymal enhancement extending from medulla to cortex in a striated pattern, with significant perinephric fat stranding and thickening of Gerota's fascia. The right kidney is mildly enlarged at 12.8 cm in long axis.
2. No obstructing calculus, nephrolithiasis, hydronephrosis, or hydroureter identified. No ureteral filling defect.
3. No focal renal abscess, liquefaction, or gas-forming infection to suggest emphysematous pyelonephritis or evolving renal abscess.
4. Left kidney normal in size, morphology, and enhancement. No perinephric stranding.
5. Bladder is unremarkable. Uterus and ovaries within normal limits. No pelvic free fluid or lymphadenopathy.
6. Impression: Uncomplicated right pyelonephritis. No drainable collection, obstruction, or gas-forming infection identified.
