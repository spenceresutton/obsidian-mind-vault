# SSTI Case 7: Purulent SSTI — Consider Withholding Antibiotics Post-Drainage

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Spencer

## Prompt

SS is a 29-year-old male with no significant past medical history who presents to the emergency department with a 4-day history of a painful, enlarging lump on the right forearm. He noticed a small area of redness that progressed to a tender, fluctuant mass. He denies fever, chills, or systemic symptoms throughout. He endorses no immunosuppression, no prior MRSA infections, and no recent hospitalizations.

On presentation, vitals are: T 36.9°C, HR 74, BP 118/72, RR 14, SpO2 99% on room air. Exam reveals a 2.5 cm fluctuant abscess on the dorsal right forearm with surrounding erythema of less than 1 cm. The abscess is unilocular and well-demarcated on palpation.

Incision and drainage is performed. Approximately 4 mL of purulent material is expressed and the wound is irrigated and loosely packed.

**Ht:** 5'11"   **Wt:** 78 kg   **BMI:** 24.3   **Baseline SCr:** 0.9 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Purulent SSTI (abscess) s/p successful I&D
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):* Incision and drainage is the primary treatment. Antibiotic therapy may be withheld in an otherwise healthy, immunocompetent patient with a simple, small abscess and no systemic signs after successful source control. If antibiotics are prescribed to reduce the risk of treatment failure and recurrence: TMP-SMX DS 1 tablet BID x5 days or doxycycline 100 mg PO BID x5 days. Either is appropriate.
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):* Patient should return immediately if fever, expanding erythema, or worsening pain develop — any of these warrants reassessment and empiric anti-MRSA therapy. Adjust to culture-directed therapy when results return.
