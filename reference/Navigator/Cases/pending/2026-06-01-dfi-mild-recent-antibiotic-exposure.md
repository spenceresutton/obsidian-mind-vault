# SSTI Case 12: Mild Diabetic Foot Infection with Recent Antibiotic Exposure

**Difficulty:** medium
**Author:** Claude

## Prompt

SS is a 68-year-old female with a past medical history significant for type 2 diabetes mellitus, hypertension, and peripheral neuropathy who presents to her primary care clinic reporting redness and swelling around a wound on the plantar surface of her left foot that she first noticed 5 days ago. She has not been soaking her foot and denies fever or chills. She was treated 3 weeks ago with a 7-day course of ciprofloxacin for a urinary tract infection. She has no prior MRSA infections, no recent hospitalization, and no penetrating trauma to the foot.

On exam, vitals are: T 37.1°C, HR 82, BP 134/78, RR 14, SpO2 97% on room air. She has a 1.5 cm ulcer at the first metatarsal head with surrounding erythema extending approximately 1.5 cm from the wound margin. The wound has a small amount of serous drainage. No purulence. No deep probing to bone on exam. Sensation is markedly diminished in the foot bilaterally consistent with known neuropathy. She takes metformin, lisinopril, and amlodipine. She has no known drug allergies.

**Ht:** 5'3"   **Wt:** 76 kg   **BMI:** 30.1   **Baseline SCr:** 0.9 mg/dL   **Allergies:** NKDA

## Imaging

### X-Ray Left Foot — AP, Lateral, Oblique

1. Soft tissue swelling adjacent to the first metatarsal head.
2. No cortical destruction or irregularity of the first metatarsal or proximal phalanx.
3. No periosteal reaction.
4. No subcutaneous emphysema or foreign body.
5. Impression: Soft tissue swelling at the first metatarsal head without radiographic evidence of osteomyelitis. Correlation with clinical exam and probe-to-bone test recommended; MRI should be considered if clinical suspicion for osteomyelitis persists despite negative plain films.

## Expected Output

- **Presentation:** Mild diabetic foot infection — localized, limited cellulitis without systemic signs; oral antibiotic therapy appropriate; no osteomyelitis on imaging; no MRSA risk factors despite recent antibiotic exposure
- **Antibiotic Initiation** *(if applicable):* Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125 mg PO BID x7–14 days. Oral therapy targeting aerobic gram-positive cocci (streptococci, MSSA) is appropriate for mild DFI. Recent ciprofloxacin exposure for UTI does not constitute an MRSA risk factor for DFI and does not change the drug class recommendation. **No anti-MRSA coverage indicated** — no prior MRSA SSTI, no penetrating trauma, no healthcare-associated exposure. Podiatry consult recommended for wound assessment, offloading, and ulcer management.
- **Treatment Optimization** *(if applicable):* Obtain deep wound culture (ideally tissue biopsy or curettage rather than superficial swab) for culture-directed step-down. Re-evaluate at 48–72h; if erythema is advancing or systemic signs develop, reassess severity and consider hospitalization. MRI of the left foot should be considered if clinical improvement is not evident at follow-up, given risk of underlying osteomyelitis in diabetic foot ulcers at pressure points.

**INCORRECT:**
- Vancomycin or anti-MRSA therapy — no MRSA risk factors are present; recent ciprofloxacin for UTI is not an MRSA risk factor for DFI
- Anti-pseudomonal coverage (pip/tazo, cefepime) — not indicated for mild DFI without maceration, soaking, or other Pseudomonas risk factors
- Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics — mild DFI without systemic signs can be managed with oral therapy in the outpatient setting
