# SJN Renal Dose Adjustment Policy

**Approved:** 02/2026 (last revised 02/2026, next review 02/2027). Policy ID 19515086. Applies to Covenant Health system including SJN Nashua.

**Pharmacist authority:** Pharmacists adjust dose and/or frequency for renally-cleared drugs without contacting the prescriber. If renal function dictates discontinuation, pharmacist contacts provider. If renal function improves and a prior dose reduction was made, pharmacist may optimize the dose upward without contacting the provider.

**SCr must be current (within 72 hours).** Pharmacist may order an SCr if not already ordered.

**Monitoring frequency:** At least every 24 hours.

---

## CrCl Calculation Notes

- Use Cockcroft-Gault for CrCl estimation
- Pip/tazo: refer to the Extended Infusion Protocol (not this table)
- Aminoglycosides (amikacin, gentamicin, tobramycin): refer to Aminoglycosides Policy

---

## CRRT Dosing Framework

CRRT clearance depends on effluent flow rate, filter type, and replacement mode. Recommendations assume high-flux dialyzers at effluent flow rates of 20–25 mL/kg/hour (~1,500–3,000 mL/hr) unless otherwise noted.

| CRRT Mode | Use This Column |
|-----------|----------------|
| SCUF | CRRT or CVVH dosing |
| CVVH | CRRT or CVVHD dosing |
| CVVHD | CRRT or CVVHD dosing |

General CRRT principles: Loading doses recommended; use usual dose initially; monitor for neurotoxicity and accumulation; consider oral alternatives where bioavailability is adequate.

## Antimicrobial Renal Dosing Reference

### Amoxicillin-Clavulanate
| CrCl | Dose |
|------|------|
| >30 | No adjustment |
| <30 | **Do not use 875mg formulation** — use 500mg q12h |
| HD | 500mg q24h |

*Note: For oral step-down in severe infections, consider q8h dosing.*

### Aztreonam
| CrCl | Dose (1g q8h) | Dose (2g q6–8h) |
|------|--------------|-----------------|
| >30 | No adjustment | No adjustment |
| 10–<30 | 1g q12h | 2g q12h |
| <10 | 1g q24h | 2g q24h |

### Cefazolin
| CrCl | Dose |
|------|------|
| >30 | No adjustment |
| 10–<30 | 2g q12h |
| <10 | 1g q24h |
| CRRT (CVVHD) | 2g single dose, then 1g q8h or 2g q12h |
| CRRT (CVVH) | 2g q12h |
| HD | 1g every dialysis day (or thrice weekly after dialysis: 2–3g) |
| CAPD | 1g q24h |

### Cefdinir
| CrCl | Dose |
|------|------|
| >30 | No adjustment |
| <30 | 300mg daily |
| CRRT | 300mg q12h |
| HD | 300mg three times/week after dialysis |

### Cefepime

## Key Teaching Points for Case Design

1. **Cefepime adjustment starts at CrCl <60, not <30** — This is the most case-relevant threshold. A patient with CrCl 45 needs cefepime adjusted to q12h. Many providers/cases assume the threshold is 30 (like most other agents). Cases testing renal dosing with cefepime should use CrCl 30–60 as the test zone.

2. **Ertapenem halved at CrCl <30** — Ertapenem 500mg q24h at CrCl <30. Cases presenting ertapenem in a patient with CrCl 22 should reflect the adjusted dose.

3. **Levofloxacin 250mg UTI exception** — At CrCl <20, levofloxacin 250mg for uncomplicated UTI requires no adjustment. For all other indications, q48h dosing applies. This is testable: a case with CrCl 15 and uncomplicated UTI on levofloxacin 250mg — no renal adjustment required.

4. **TMP/SMX reduced, not eliminated at CrCl 15–30** — 50% dose is appropriate. At <15: 25% dose. Cases presenting renal impairment and TMP/SMX (e.g., prostatitis step-down or uncomplicated UTI) should include these adjustments.

5. **Fluconazole threshold is CrCl <50** — 50% dose reduction. Many cases with candidemia or antifungal therapy involve patients with CKD; the threshold here is higher than most antibiotics.

6. **Amox-clav 875mg contraindicated at CrCl <30** — Downgrade to 500mg q12h. A case offering amox-clav 875mg BID as a step-down option in a patient with CrCl 25 is prescribing an excluded formulation.

7. **Pharmacist autonomy — no need to contact provider for dose adjustments** — The SJN protocol explicitly authorizes pharmacists to adjust doses without a provider call. Cases should reflect this workflow: Navigator recommends the renal-adjusted dose; the pharmacist implements it.

8. **SCr must be within 72 hours** — A case with a creatinine result from 4 days ago is not sufficient for renal dosing decisions. Navigator should flag stale SCr.

9. **CRRT changes both dose and frequency** — CRRT patients are not simply "CrCl 0." Cefepime 2g q12h on CRRT; meropenem 1g q12h on CVVH (1g q8h on CVVHD); ertapenem 1g q24h on CRRT (full dose, not reduced). Cases involving CRRT should reflect these specific recommendations.

10. **Pip/tazo renal adjustment lives in the extended infusion protocol, not this table** — For pip/tazo dosing in CKD, see the Extended Infusion Policy.
