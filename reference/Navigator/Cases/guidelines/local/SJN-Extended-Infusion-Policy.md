# SJN Extended Infusion Beta-Lactam Policy

## Why Extended Infusion

Beta-lactams are time-dependent killers — efficacy tracks %T>MIC, not peak concentration. Extending infusion time from 30 minutes to 3–4 hours maintains drug concentration above the MIC for a greater fraction of the dosing interval. Critically ill patients with augmented renal clearance, high volume of distribution from resuscitation, or infections with higher-MIC organisms benefit most.

Baxter IV fluid shortage (~2022) had driven many institutions to IV push beta-lactams. SJN's pharmacy (Nick Cotoia, PharmD) proposed and P&T approved in February 2026 that cefepime and meropenem be removed from IV push and reverted to IVPB infusion — setting the foundation for extended infusion use on ID recommendation.

## Piperacillin-Tazobactam (Zosyn) — Active Protocol (Approved 03/2026)

**Pharmacist-driven:** Pharmacists convert all pip/tazo orders from traditional to extended infusion by default per this protocol, except where explicitly excluded.

### Exclusions (do NOT convert to extended infusion)
- One-time dose ordered in the Emergency Department
- One-time dose ordered for surgical prophylaxis or pre-op

### Loading Dose

All patients receive a loading dose of **4.5g IV over 30 minutes** before the extended infusion regimen begins. In patients requiring rapid attainment of therapeutic concentrations (e.g., septic shock), administer the loading dose first without delay.

### Standard Extended Infusion Dosing Table

| Patient Situation | Traditional Dose | → Extended Infusion |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| CrCl >20 mL/min or CRRT | 2.25g q8h | **3.375g q8h over 4 hours** |
| CrCl <20 mL/min | 2.25g q6h | **3.375g q12h over 4 hours** |
| Peritoneal dialysis or hemodialysis | 2.25g q12h | **3.375g q12h over 4 hours** |

### Special Circumstances — Use 4.5g q8h over 4 hours

For the following patients, escalate to the higher extended infusion regimen:
- Sepsis
- Critical care patients with severe or deep-seated infections
- Infections with MIC >16 mg/L
- Obesity: weight >120 kg or BMI >40
- CrCl >120 mL/min (augmented renal clearance)
- Enhanced drug clearance (e.g., cystic fibrosis)

**Dose:** 4.5g IV q8h; infuse over 4 hours

*Note: CrCl-adjusted version of the high-dose regimen:*
- CrCl >20 or CRRT: 4.5g q8h over 4 hours
- CrCl <20: 4.5g q12h over 4 hours

## Cefepime and Meropenem — IVPB Transition (P&T Feb 2026)

**Status:** P&T approved to remove cefepime and meropenem from IV push; transition to IVPB infusion (30 min). Dosing unchanged. Epic orders updated to reflect IVPB settings.

**Why this matters:**
- **Cefepime IV push risks:** Increased neurotoxicity, increased neutropenia, higher treatment failure rate (48% vs. 21%, p=0.084 — Bland et al. 2025)
- **Meropenem extended infusion benefit:** Shorter time to clinical stability (20.4h vs. 66.2h for IV push, p=0.01); shorter length of stay

**Pathway to extended infusion for cefepime/meropenem:** ID may recommend extended infusion dosing for cefepime or meropenem in appropriate cases (e.g., resistant organisms, critical illness, high-MIC pathogens). The IVPB order setting allows this recommendation to be entered without an additional formulary exception.

Standard IVPB infusion for these agents at SJN:
- **Cefepime:** 30-minute infusion (standard IVPB)
- **Meropenem:** 30-minute infusion (standard IVPB); extended infusion (3–4 hours) on ID recommendation

## Key Teaching Points for Case Design

1. **Pip/tazo is always extended infusion at SJN** — Cases should not present pip/tazo as a 30-minute infusion unless it is a one-time ED or surgical prophylaxis dose. The standard SJN pip/tazo regimen is 3.375g q8h over 4 hours (or 4.5g q8h for high-risk patients).

2. **Loading dose precedes extended infusion** — A case testing pip/tazo initiation should include the 4.5g loading dose over 30 minutes before the extended infusion regimen. Jumping straight to 3.375g q8h over 4h without a loading dose delays time to therapeutic levels.

3. **Sepsis and critical illness trigger the higher dose** — 3.375g q8h is the standard dose; 4.5g q8h is the high-risk dose for sepsis, ICU patients, high-MIC organisms, obesity, or augmented clearance. A case presenting a septic shock patient on pip/tazo should use 4.5g q8h, not 3.375g.

4. **CrCl <20 halves the frequency, not the dose** — At CrCl <20, the renal-adjusted regimen is 3.375g q12h (or 4.5g q12h for special circumstances), not a dose reduction. Cases with severe CKD should reflect this interval extension.

5. **Cefepime IV push is being eliminated at SJN** — IV push cefepime is an anti-pattern going forward. Cases should model cefepime as a 30-minute IVPB at minimum, with extended infusion available on ID recommendation for complex cases.

6. **Extended infusion does not increase the daily dose — it redistributes it** — The PK benefit comes from maintaining time above MIC. A case asking about extended infusion rationale should focus on %T>MIC, not peak concentration or AUC.
