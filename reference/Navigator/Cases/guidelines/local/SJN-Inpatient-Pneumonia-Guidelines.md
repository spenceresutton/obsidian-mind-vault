# SJN Inpatient Pneumonia Guidelines

## Introduction

These guidelines prioritize reducing IV catheter-related adverse events, costs, and resource burden. Core operational principles:

- IV therapy reserved for: patients unable to take PO, susceptibility profiles precluding oral agents, critically ill patients, or tenuous hemodynamics
- Transition to oral therapy when feasible
- Antimicrobial choice adjusted based on respiratory and blood culture/susceptibility results

---

## Definitions

| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| CAP | Pneumonia presenting or incubating at time of admission; occurring within 48 hours of hospital admission |
| HAP | Pneumonia not incubating at time of admission; occurring ≥48 hours after admission |
| VAP | Pneumonia occurring >48 hours after endotracheal intubation |

---

## CAP: Empiric Inpatient Treatment

Treatment is determined by two decisions: (1) Is this severe CAP? (2) Which risk factors are present? The algorithm below reflects the flowchart from the SJN PDF.

**Severity definition:** Severe CAP = 1 major criterion OR ≥3 minor criteria (2007 IDSA/ATS criteria — same as national guideline; see table in national guideline note).

**Risk factors (either qualifies for escalation):**
1. Prior respiratory isolation of MRSA or *P. aeruginosa* **within the past year** (<1 year)
2. Recent hospitalization **AND** IV antibiotics received **within the past 90 days**

---

### Non-Severe CAP Regimens

| Risk Scenario | Regimen |
|---------------|---------|
| **No risk factors** | Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily^b + Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days^c |
| **MRSA respiratory isolation ≤1 year** | Ceftriaxone 2g IV daily^b + Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days^c + Vancomycin 15mg/kg IV^d,e |
| **Pseudo respiratory isolation ≤1 year** | Cefepime 2g IV q8h^d,e + Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days^c |
| **Hospitalization + IV abx ≤90 days** OR **both MRSA + Pseudo isolation ≤1 year** | Cefepime 2g IV q8h^d,e + Azithromycin 500mg PO daily × 3 days^c + Vancomycin 15mg/kg IV^d,e |

**Critical distinctions:**
- MRSA isolation alone → keep ceftriaxone (standard beta-lactam), add vancomycin
- Pseudo isolation alone → swap ceftriaxone for cefepime, keep azithromycin, no vancomycin
- Hospitalization + IV abx (90d) → cefepime + azithromycin + vancomycin (covers both)
- Oral abx alone do NOT qualify — requires IV antibiotics + hospitalization together

---

### Severe CAP Regimens

Severe CAP follows the same risk-stratified branching with the same empiric agents. (Dosing and agents identical to non-severe branches above; severity drives additional monitoring and site-of-care decisions, not a different drug list at SJN.)

---

### Therapeutic Alternatives

| Preferred | Alternative |
|-----------|------------|
| Cefepime | Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q8h |
| Ceftriaxone | Levofloxacin 750mg PO/IV q24h |
| Azithromycin | Doxycycline 100mg PO/IV q12h |
| Vancomycin | Linezolid 600mg PO/IV q12h |

---

### Duration

**5 days with clinical stability**

Clinical stability = resolution of abnormalities in HR, RR, BP, O₂ sat, temperature, mental status, and ability to eat.

---

**Key local nuance vs. national guideline:** The 2019 IDSA/ATS guideline recommends cultures only (no empiric Pseudo coverage) for non-severe CAP with the 90-day IV abx history. SJN empirically covers both MRSA and Pseudo with that risk factor regardless of severity. Cases test adherence to SJN, not the national baseline.

---

## CAP: De-escalation Rules

These are explicit de-escalation triggers — all are required, not optional:

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Atypical organisms ruled out | Stop azithromycin or doxycycline |
| No MRSA on respiratory cultures OR MRSA nares PCR negative within last 72 hours | Discontinue vancomycin (or linezolid) |
| Pseudomonas aeruginosa ruled out on cultures | De-escalate from cefepime/pip-tazo to standard beta-lactam |
| Known pathogen identified | Direct therapy based on susceptibility results |

---

## CAP: IV-to-PO Transition

Transition when:
- Patient clinically identified as stable
- Tolerating PO medications

---

## HAP/VAP: Empiric Treatment

Duration: **7 days**

| Coverage | Preferred Agent | Alternative |
|---------|----------------|------------|
| *P. aeruginosa* (choose one) | Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV extended infusion | — |
| | Cefepime 2g IV q8h | — |
| | Meropenem 1g IV q8h (if recent MDRO history) | — |
| MRSA (choose one) | Vancomycin (pharmacy-dosed)* | Linezolid 600mg IV q12h** |

*Discontinue vancomycin if MRSA nares PCR negative.
**Restricted antimicrobial.

## Key SJN-Specific Teaching Points for Case Design

1. **Empiric dual coverage (MRSA + Pseudo) in non-severe CAP** — SJN triggers empiric cefepime + vancomycin for any patient with the 90-day IV abx + hospitalization risk factor, even without ICU-level severity. This is more aggressive than the national guideline and is the specific knowledge being tested in CAP-05 and CAP-06.

2. **MRSA nares PCR as de-escalation gate** — De-escalation of vancomycin is explicitly tied to nares PCR negativity (within 72h), not just clinical improvement. A case where vancomycin is continued despite a negative nares PCR is an anti-pattern at SJN.

3. **Prior respiratory isolation window is 1 year** — SJN specifies <1 year for prior MRSA or Pseudo respiratory isolation. The national guideline does not specify a window. Cases can test whether candidates recognize a 14-month-old Pseudo culture does not trigger empiric coverage under SJN criteria.

4. **Oral abx alone do NOT trigger risk escalation** — Risk factors require IV antibiotics + hospitalization. Recent oral antibiotic course (CAP-04) is a red herring; it does not meet the SJN escalation threshold.

5. **HAP/VAP: 7-day duration explicit** — Consistent with 2016 IDSA/ATS HAP/VAP recommendation.

6. **Vancomycin is pharmacy-dosed** — Cases should not specify a vancomycin dose; SJN pharmacy manages dosing. This is operationally correct at SJN; generated cases should reflect this.

7. **Linezolid is restricted** — Cases can reference linezolid as an alternative but should note it requires ID or restriction approval at SJN.

8. **Meropenem for recent MDRO history** — HAP/VAP cases with prior MDRO exposure should escalate to meropenem over cefepime or pip-tazo.
