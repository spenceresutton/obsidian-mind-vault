# SJN IV to Enteral (IV to PO) Policy

**Status:** Pending final P&T approval (effective upon approval). Authored by Nicholas Cotoia, PharmD. Policy ID 19702779.

Pharmacist-driven substitution from IV to equivalent oral formulation. Authorized under P&T policy — pharmacist discontinues IV, enters PO order under the original prescriber's name, adds "IV to PO per P&T Policy" to administration comments.

---

## Universal Inclusion Criteria (all must be met)

- Tolerating diet more advanced than clear liquids OR tolerating PO medications
- Received ≥24 hours of IV antimicrobials
- If culture available: pathogen is sensitive to the proposed oral agent
- Clinically stable:
  - Afebrile for ≥24 hours (temperature <100.4°F)
  - Hemodynamically stable: SBP >90 and not on vasopressors, HR <100
  - WBC trending toward normal

## Universal Exclusion Criteria (any one disqualifies)

- Nausea or vomiting within past 24 hours
- Unable to swallow
- Receiving continuous nasogastric suctioning OR NG residual <200 mL
- Active GI bleed
- Pre-op (NPO)
- Immunocompromised or neutropenic
- Gastrectomy, ileus, gastric outlet obstruction, bowel obstruction, or altered GI absorption
- Allergy to the proposed PO alternative
- Indication not eligible for conversion (see below)

---

## Indications — Automatic Conversion vs. Provider Contact

### Auto-Convert (pharmacist converts without contacting provider)
- Bronchitis
- Pneumonia (CAP / HAP / VAP)
- UTI / Pyelonephritis
- Acute Exacerbation of COPD
- GBS Prophylaxis

### Remain IV — Do NOT Convert Automatically
Contact provider to discuss at 48 hours due to location or severity of infection:

| Indication | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| Brain abscess / CNS infection | CNS penetration requirements |
| Candidemia treated <7 days | Insufficient oral data at early phase |
| Clostridium difficile infection | IV not typically used; CDI-specific |
| Cystic fibrosis | Complex PK considerations |
| Endocarditis | IV required for adequate tissue levels |
| Endophthalmitis | Ocular penetration |
| Meningitis | CNS penetration |
| Orbital cellulitis | Proximity to CNS |
| Osteomyelitis | Bone penetration, duration considerations |
| Post-op prophylaxis / on-call doses | Single-dose indication; IV appropriate |
| Septic arthritis | Joint penetration |
| Sepsis treated <7 days | Premature switch in active systemic illness |

### Contact Provider First (not auto-convert, not forbidden)
- Cellulitis
- Wound infection
- Any indication not listed above

### Agent-Specific Notes

**†Ceftriaxone → Cefpodoxime:**
- Additional inclusion: blood cultures with no growth for ≥48 hours
- Additional exclusion: **urinary catheter present** — do NOT convert if catheter in place

**‡Ciprofloxacin:** Do NOT administer via feeding tube.

**§Levofloxacin:** For patients on continuous enteral nutrition, hold EN for 2 hours before and 2 hours after levofloxacin administration.

## Pharmacist Workflow

1. Review active IV antimicrobials for eligible indications
2. Confirm indication is auto-convert eligible
3. Verify patient meets all inclusion criteria and none of the exclusion criteria
4. Check culture data — confirm pathogen sensitive to proposed oral agent (or no culture yet, clinical judgment)
5. Confirm no allergy to PO alternative
6. Discontinue IV order; enter PO order under original prescriber with "IV to PO per P&T Policy" in administration comments
7. Time PO dose based on last IV dose timing
8. Apply renal dose adjustments per the Renal Dose Adjustment protocol
9. Follow patient for tolerability and clinical stability

## Key Teaching Points for Case Design

1. **≥24h of IV therapy is required before auto-conversion** — A case where the patient has been on IV antibiotics for only 12 hours and is clinically stable does not yet qualify for pharmacist-driven switch. The provider must order or the pharmacist must wait.

2. **Ceftriaxone → cefpodoxime but NOT with a urinary catheter** — This is a specific and testable SJN restriction. A CAUTI case where ceftriaxone is being used and the catheter is still in place: conversion to cefpodoxime is explicitly excluded. Remove the catheter first, then reassess.

3. **Sepsis <7 days is a hold indicator** — Patients presenting with sepsis are not candidates for IV-to-PO conversion until they have received at least 7 days of IV therapy. A case presenting a septic patient at day 4 who is improving but not yet at 7 days: continue IV.

4. **Osteomyelitis and endocarditis remain IV** — Despite clinical improvement, these indications require provider discussion. A case with bone or valve infection should not auto-convert.

5. **Vasopressor use or hemodynamic instability = not eligible** — SBP >90 and no vasopressors is an explicit inclusion criterion. A case with a patient on low-dose norepinephrine should not be switched to PO regardless of clinical appearance.

6. **Neutropenia or immunocompromise excludes conversion** — Even if otherwise stable, immunocompromised or neutropenic patients require provider contact before switch.

7. **Ciprofloxacin through a feeding tube is prohibited** — IV ciprofloxacin should not be switched to enteral administration via NG/OG tube. Levofloxacin is the preferred oral FQ for tube-fed patients (with EN hold).

8. **Amox-clav dose match**: Unasyn 1.5g q6h → Augmentin 500/125mg q12h. This is NOT a 1:1 frequency match — the oral regimen is q12h, not q6h. Cases testing conversion math should use this table.
