# SJN Inpatient Clostridioides difficile Infection (CDI) Guidelines

**Source:** St. Joseph Hospital Department of Pharmacy Antimicrobial Stewardship
**Adapted from:** SHEA, IDSA, and ACG consensus recommendations

---

## Introduction

Key principles:
- Discontinue unnecessary antibiotics whenever possible; shorten courses, de-escalate, and preferentially select lower CDI-risk agents
- Clindamycin, later-generation cephalosporins, and fluoroquinolones carry increased CDI risk
- Anti-peristaltic agents contraindicated in fulminant CDI
- Test only patients with active diarrhea (≥3 liquid stools within 24 hours), ileus, or colitis
- Test-of-cure is NOT recommended — both toxin assays and PCR may remain positive for weeks after treatment

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| CDI | Diarrhea or ileus/colitis with stool test positive for *C. difficile* toxins, detection of toxigenic *C. difficile*, or colonoscopic/histopathologic findings revealing pseudomembrane colitis |
| Nonsevere CDI | WBC ≤15,000 cells/µL AND serum creatinine <1.5 mg/dL |
| Fulminant CDI | Hypotension, shock, ileus, or megacolon attributed to CDI |
| Recurrent CDI | Resolution of symptoms on therapy followed by reappearance within 2–8 weeks of completing treatment |
| FMT | Fecal microbiota transplant |
| PCR | Polymerase chain reaction (nucleic acid amplification test for *C. difficile*) |
| Toxin IA | Toxin enzyme immunoassay — detects free toxin A and/or B |

---

## Diagnosis Algorithm

| Test Result | Interpretation | Action |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| PCR (−) | No CDI present | Do not treat |
| PCR (+), Antigen (−), Toxin (−) | CDI colonization; very low organism levels; unlikely to cause symptoms | Do not treat. Place in enteric isolation |
| PCR (+), Antigen (+), Toxin (+) | Toxigenic CDI present | Treat as CDI |
| PCR (+), Antigen (+), Toxin (−) | CDI may be present; likely no toxin production or low-level CDI; may represent colonization or recent treatment | Treat if high clinical suspicion (severe, non-resolving, or unexplained diarrhea). Place in enteric isolation |
| PCR (+), Antigen (−), Toxin (+) | Indeterminate | Repeat test |

## Treatment

> **Critical notes:**
> - Vancomycin must be administered **orally** — IV formulation does not achieve adequate luminal concentrations
> - **Fidaxomicin (Dificid®) requires Infectious Disease approval prior to use** (restricted antimicrobial)

| Presentation | Preferred Treatment | Alternative Treatment |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **Initial Episode** | Fidaxomicin 200 mg PO BID × 10 days | Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H × 10 days |
| **First Recurrence** | Fidaxomicin 200 mg PO BID × 10 days **OR** Fidaxomicin extended-pulsed: 200 mg PO BID × 5 days, then 200 mg PO every other day × 20 days | Vancomycin taper: 125 mg Q6H × 10–14 days → 125 mg Q12H × 7 days → 125 mg daily × 7 days → 125 mg Q48–72H × 2–8 weeks **OR** Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q6H × 10 days |
| **Second or Subsequent Recurrence** | Options (no preferred order): Fidaxomicin standard or extended-pulsed; Vancomycin tapered/pulsed. Consider novel microbiome replacement therapy after completing CDI treatment. | Vancomycin 125 mg Q6H × 10 days, then rifaximin 400 mg Q8H × 20 days |
| **Fulminant CDI** | Vancomycin 500 mg PO/NG Q6H +/− Metronidazole 500 mg IV Q8H for ≥10 days (extend based on clinical status). If ileus: add Vancomycin retention enema 500 mg in 100 mL sterile water Q6H. Metronidazole IV may be discontinued once oral vancomycin passes reliably. **Fidaxomicin is NOT recommended for fulminant CDI.** **ID & Surgery consults strongly recommended.** | — |

**Duration note:** Durations may be extended on a case-by-case basis for patients on concomitant antibiotics and those at high risk for recurrence (age ≥65 years, immunocompromised, or severe CDI on presentation).

**Fidaxomicin discharge planning:** If patient will likely be discharged before completing a 10-day course, investigate outpatient insurance coverage immediately. Patient assistance programs may be available. If coverage cannot be confirmed, plan to complete with oral vancomycin.

## Key Footnotes & Restricted Antimicrobials

| Agent | Restriction / Note |
|-------|-------------------|
| Fidaxomicin (Dificid®) | **Restricted antimicrobial — requires Infectious Disease approval prior to use** |
| Metronidazole (oral) | Reserved for nonsevere initial CDI only when no other options are available; inferior to vancomycin and fidaxomicin; not recommended for recurrent or fulminant CDI |
| Vancomycin (IV) | IV formulation does NOT treat CDI — does not achieve adequate luminal concentrations. Must be administered orally or via retention enema |
| Rebyota® / Vowst® | FDA-approved microbiome replacement therapies for patients with ≥1 CDI recurrence. Timing relative to end of CDI therapy must be observed |

## Prevention

### Oral Vancomycin Prophylaxis

| Indication | Regimen | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Primary Prophylaxis | Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q24H or Q12H | Evidence sparse. Consider in profoundly immunosuppressed patients receiving systemic antibiotics |
| Secondary Prophylaxis | Vancomycin 125 mg PO Q24H or Q12H during subsequent systemic antibiotic courses | Consider in patients with recent CDI history at high risk (age ≥65, immunocompromised, or severe CDI within 3 months). Continue for up to 5–7 days after systemic antibiotic completion. Appropriateness guided by spectrum of antibiotic used. |
| Suppressive Therapy | Oral vancomycin (dose and duration individualized) | For patients with recurrent CDI not candidates for or who have relapsed following commercially available fecal microbiota products, or who require ongoing/frequent antibiotic courses |

> **Probiotics:** Most recent guidelines do not recommend routine use of probiotics for primary or secondary prevention of CDI. Routine use is particularly discouraged in immunocompromised patients.

## Novel Microbiome Replacement Therapies

Rebyota® and Vowst® are the only FDA-approved microbiome replacement options for patients with ≥1 CDI recurrence.

| Agent | Route | Dose & Timing | Special Considerations |
|-------|-------|--------------|----------------------|
| Rebyota® (fecal microbiota, live-jslm) | Rectal | 150 mL enema × 1 dose. Administer 24–72h after completing CDI therapy | Limited data in IBD and immunosuppression |
| Vowst® (fecal microbiota spores, live-brpk) | Oral | 4 capsules PO daily × 3 days on empty stomach after bowel purge*. Administer 48–96h after completing CDI therapy | Contraindicated without oral access or in patients with pill dysphagia. Capsules must be swallowed whole. Limited data in IBD and immunosuppression |

*Bowel purge: Magnesium citrate 296 mL ≥8 hours before first dose (CrCl ≥60 mL/min); GoLYTELY 250 mL (CrCl <60 mL/min)

> ⚠️ **BEZLOTOXUMAB (Zinplava®) WAS DISCONTINUED ON JANUARY 31, 2025 AND IS NO LONGER AVAILABLE.** Current CDI guidelines have not yet been updated to reflect this discontinuation.

---

## Infection Control

Contact precautions (gown and gloves) for all patients with known or suspected CDI. Hand hygiene with soap and water preferred over alcohol-based hand rubs — *C. difficile* spores are not reliably eliminated by alcohol. Treatment decision does not dictate infection precautions.
