# 2014 IDSA SSTI Guidelines

Stevens DL, Bisno AL, Chambers HF, et al. *Practice Guidelines for the Diagnosis and Management of Skin and Soft Tissue Infections: 2014 Update by the Infectious Diseases Society of America.* Clin Infect Dis. 2014;59(2):e10–e52.

This national guideline supplies the **reasoning** behind SSTI case calibration. Where it diverges from SJN practice, [[reference/Navigator/Cases/guidelines/local/SJN-SSTI-Guidelines]] governs the expected output (see [[brain/Case Style Guide#Guideline Hierarchy]]).

## Management Algorithm (Figure 1)

SSTIs branch first on **purulence**, then on **severity** (mild / moderate / severe).

### Nonpurulent (necrotizing infection / cellulitis / erysipelas)

| Severity | Management |
|----------|-----------|
| **Mild** | Oral Rx: penicillin VK, cephalosporin, dicloxacillin, or clindamycin |
| **Moderate** | IV Rx: penicillin, ceftriaxone, cefazolin, or clindamycin |
| **Severe** | Emergent surgical inspection/debridement + rule out necrotizing process; empiric vancomycin PLUS piperacillin/tazobactam |

**Severe nonpurulent** = failed oral therapy + systemic signs of infection, OR immunocompromised, OR clinical signs of deeper infection (bullae, skin sloughing, hypotension, organ dysfunction). Defined therapy for necrotizing infections:
- Monomicrobial *S. pyogenes* → penicillin PLUS clindamycin
- *Clostridium* sp. → penicillin PLUS clindamycin
- Polymicrobial → vancomycin PLUS piperacillin/tazobactam

### Purulent (furuncle / carbuncle / abscess)

| Severity | Management |
|----------|-----------|
| **Mild** | I&D only |
| **Moderate** | I&D + culture & sensitivity; empiric TMP/SMX or doxycycline |
| **Severe** | I&D + culture & sensitivity; empiric vancomycin, daptomycin, linezolid, telavancin, or ceftaroline |

Defined purulent therapy — MRSA: TMP/SMX. MSSA: dicloxacillin or cephalexin.

**Severity definitions (SIRS-based):** moderate = purulent infection with systemic signs (temp >38°C, HR >90, RR >24, WBC >12,000 or <400). Severe = failed I&D + oral antibiotics, or systemic signs, or immunocompromised.

## Key Recommendations

### Erysipelas and cellulitis (Q14–15)

- Typical cellulitis **without** systemic signs (mild): agent active against streptococci.
- Cellulitis **with** systemic signs (moderate nonpurulent): systemic antibiotics indicated; MSSA coverage may be included.
- Cellulitis with **penetrating trauma, evidence of MRSA elsewhere, nasal MRSA colonization, injection drug use, or SIRS** (severe nonpurulent): cover both MRSA and streptococci. *(This is the basis for the SJN "Moderate – MRSA Risk" trigger set.)*
- Severely compromised patients (severe nonpurulent): broad-spectrum coverage may be considered — vancomycin PLUS piperacillin/tazobactam or imipenem/meropenem.
- **Recommended duration is 5 days**, extended if not improved (strong, high).

### Purulent SSTI (Q II)

- I&D is the recommended treatment for abscesses, large furuncles, carbuncles, inflamed epidermoid cysts (strong, high).
- Antibiotics as adjunct to I&D: decided on presence/absence of SIRS. Anti-MRSA recommended for carbuncles/abscesses that failed initial treatment, markedly impaired host defenses, or SIRS with hypotension (severe).

### Impetigo and ecthyma (Q I)

- Bullous/nonbullous impetigo: oral or topical antimicrobials (mupirocin or retapamulin BID × 5 days for limited lesions); oral for numerous lesions/outbreaks.
- 7-day oral regimen against *S. aureus* unless cultures yield streptococci alone.
- MRSA suspected/confirmed: doxycycline, clindamycin, or TMP/SMX.

### Recurrent abscesses (Q III)

- Search for local cause (pilonidal cyst, hidradenitis, foreign material).
- Drain and culture early; treat 5–10 days against the isolated pathogen.
- Consider a 5-day decolonization regimen (intranasal mupirocin, chlorhexidine washes, decontamination of personal items).

| Disease entity | Agent | Adult dose | Note |
|----------------|-------|-----------|------|
| MSSA SSTI | Nafcillin/oxacillin | 1–2 g q4h IV | Parenteral drug of choice; inactive against MRSA |
| MSSA SSTI | Cefazolin | 1 g q8h IV | For penicillin-allergic except immediate hypersensitivity |
| MSSA SSTI | Clindamycin | 600 mg q8h IV or 300–450 mg qid PO | Bacteriostatic; inducible resistance in MRSA |
| MSSA SSTI | Dicloxacillin / Cephalexin | 500 mg qid PO | Oral agents of choice for MSSA |
| MRSA SSTI | Vancomycin | 30 mg/kg/day in 2 divided doses IV | Parenteral drug of choice for MRSA |
| MRSA SSTI | Linezolid | 600 mg q12h IV or PO | Bacteriostatic; expensive |
| MRSA SSTI | Daptomycin | 4 mg/kg q24h IV | Bactericidal; possible myopathy |
| MRSA SSTI | Ceftaroline | 600 mg BID IV | Bactericidal |
| MRSA SSTI | Doxycycline/minocycline | 100 mg BID PO | Bacteriostatic |
| MRSA SSTI | TMP/SMX | 1–2 DS tablets BID PO | Bactericidal; limited published efficacy data |
