# 2017 IDSA/SHEA Clinical Practice Guidelines: *Clostridium difficile* Infection in Adults and Children

**Published:** Clinical Infectious Diseases, 2018;66(7):e1–e48
**Note:** Superseded in part by the 2021 IDSA/SHEA Focused Update (fidaxomicin preference for initial/recurrent CDI; bezlotoxumab). Non-addressed topics remain governed by this 2017 guideline.

---

## Severity Definitions

| Category | Criteria |
|----------|---------|
| Nonsevere | WBC ≤15,000 cells/mL AND serum creatinine <1.5 mg/dL |
| Severe | WBC >15,000 cells/mL OR serum creatinine ≥1.5 mg/dL |
| Fulminant* | Hypotension or shock, ileus, megacolon |

*Previously referred to as "severe, complicated" CDI.

---

## Treatment Recommendations (Adults — Table 1)

| Clinical Definition | Supportive Clinical Data | Recommended Treatment | Strength/Quality |
|--------------------|------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Initial episode, non-severe | WBC ≤15,000, Cr <1.5 mg/dL | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days **OR** Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days | Strong/High |
| | | Metronidazole 500 mg TID × 10–14 days if above unavailable | Weak/High |
| Initial episode, severe | WBC >15,000 OR Cr ≥1.5 mg/dL | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days **OR** Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days | Strong/High |
| Initial episode, fulminant | Hypotension/shock, ileus, megacolon | Vancomycin 500 mg QID PO or NG. If ileus: add rectal vancomycin (500 mg in ~100 mL NS per rectum Q6H). IV metronidazole 500 mg q8h if ileus present. | Strong/Moderate (oral VAN); Weak/Low (rectal VAN); Strong/Moderate (IV metro) |
| | | Surgical management if severely ill: subtotal colectomy with preservation of rectum. Diverting loop ileostomy with colonic lavage as alternative. | Strong/Moderate; Weak/Low |
| First recurrence | — | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days if metronidazole used for initial episode | Weak/Low |
| | | Vancomycin tapered/pulsed regimen if standard regimen used for initial episode | Weak/Low |
| | | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days if vancomycin was used for initial episode | Weak/Moderate |
| Second or subsequent recurrence | — | Vancomycin tapered/pulsed | Weak/Low |
| | | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days → rifaximin 400 mg TID × 20 days | Weak/Low |
| | | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days | Weak/Low |
| | | Fecal microbiota transplantation (after ≥2 appropriate antibiotic treatment attempts) | Strong/Moderate |

**Vancomycin tapered/pulsed example:** 125 mg QID × 10–14 days → 125 mg BID × 7 days → 125 mg daily × 7 days → 125 mg every 2–3 days × 2–8 weeks.

## Ancillary Treatment Strategies

- **Discontinue the inciting antibiotic(s) as soon as possible** — strong recommendation, moderate quality of evidence
- Anti-peristaltic agents: avoid in active CDI
- Empiric treatment appropriate when substantial delay in lab confirmation is expected or for fulminant CDI (weak recommendation)

---

## Diagnosis

### Testing Population

- Patients with unexplained, new-onset ≥3 unformed stools in 24 hours are the preferred testing population (weak recommendation, very low quality of evidence)
- Do NOT test stool from asymptomatic patients, except for epidemiological studies (strong recommendation, moderate quality of evidence)

### Test Method

- Use a stool toxin test as part of a multistep algorithm: GDH plus toxin; GDH plus toxin, arbitrated by NAAT; or NAAT plus toxin (weak recommendation, low quality of evidence)
- Do not use NAAT alone when there are no pre-agreed institutional criteria for patient stool submission

### Test of Cure

- **Do NOT repeat testing within 7 days during the same episode** and do not test stool from asymptomatic patients (strong recommendation, moderate quality of evidence)

## Infection Prevention and Control

| Topic | Recommendation | Strength/Quality |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| Private room | Accommodate CDI patients in private room with dedicated toilet | Strong/Moderate |
| Gloves & gowns | Healthcare personnel must use gloves and gowns on entry | Strong/High (gloves); Strong/Moderate (gowns) |
| Contact precautions | Place suspected CDI patients on preemptive contact precautions pending test results | Strong/Moderate |
| Duration | Continue contact precautions ≥48 hours after diarrhea resolves | Weak/Low |
| Hand hygiene | Soap and water in routine settings; soap and water preferred in outbreaks/hyperendemic settings | Strong/Moderate |
| Sporicidal disinfectant | Consider daily cleaning with sporicidal agent during outbreaks or hyperendemic settings | Weak/Low |
| Antibiotic stewardship | Minimize frequency/duration of high-risk antibiotics; restrict fluoroquinolones, clindamycin, cephalosporins (except surgical prophylaxis) | Strong/Moderate |
| PPIs | Insufficient data to recommend discontinuation of PPIs as CDI prevention measure | No recommendation |

---

## FMT

- **Recommended for multiple CDI recurrences who have failed appropriate antibiotic treatments (strong recommendation, moderate quality of evidence)**
- The panel's opinion: appropriate antibiotic treatments for at least 2 recurrences (≥3 CDI episodes) should be tried prior to offering FMT
- Three safety alerts from FDA since June 2019 regarding serious adverse events from FMT (drug-resistant *E. coli*, SARS-CoV-2); use with caution

---

## Risk Factors for CDI

**Modifiable:**
- Antibiotic exposure (especially fluoroquinolones, clindamycin, 3rd/4th-generation cephalosporins, carbapenems)
- PPI/H2 blocker use (associated but causality uncertain)
- Hospitalization duration
- GI manipulation or tube feeding

**Non-modifiable:**
- Advanced age (≥65 years)
- Prior CDI
- Immunosuppression (cancer, transplant, IBD, HIV)
- Inflammatory bowel disease

---

## Pediatric Summary (Table 2 highlights)

- Initial episode, non-severe: metronidazole 7.5 mg/kg/dose TID or QID × 10 days OR vancomycin 10 mg/kg/dose QID × 10 days (max 125 mg QID) — Weak/Low
- Initial episode, severe/fulminant: vancomycin 10 mg/kg/dose QID × 10 days (max 500 mg QID) — Strong/Moderate
- First recurrence, non-severe: same as initial
- Second/subsequent recurrence: vancomycin taper/pulsed OR vancomycin × 10 days → rifaximin × 20 days OR FMT — Weak/Low
