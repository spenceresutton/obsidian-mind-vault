# 2021 ESCMID: Treatment Guidance for *Clostridioides difficile* Infection in Adults

**Published:** Clinical Microbiology and Infection, 2021;27(12):S1–S21
**Scope:** Adults only. Diagnostic strategies and prevention/control measures are addressed in separate ESCMID guidance documents.

---

## Key Changes from 2014 Guideline

- Metronidazole is **no longer recommended** for treatment of CDI when fidaxomicin or vancomycin are available
- Fidaxomicin is the **preferred agent** for initial CDI and first recurrence when available and feasible
- FMT or bezlotoxumab preferred for treatment of a second or further CDI recurrence *(bezlotoxumab now discontinued per SJN)*
- Emphasis placed on **risk for recurrence** as the primary factor determining treatment strategy, rather than disease severity alone

---

## Definitions

**Episode of CDI:** Clinical findings compatible with CDI AND evidence of *C. difficile* free toxins by EIA **OR** positive NAAT with low cycle threshold (Ct) value **OR** positive toxigenic *C. difficile* culture **OR** pseudomembranous colitis on endoscopy/autopsy.

**Diarrhoea:** ≥3 loose stools (Bristol stool scale 6–7) in 24 hours.

**Treatment response:** Resolution of diarrhoea AND formed/normal stool for the patient, WITH maintenance of resolution for the duration of therapy and ≥48h after end of treatment, AND no further requirement for CDI therapy, AND parameters of disease severity improved with no new signs of severe disease.

**Severe CDI:** Characterized by one or more at presentation:
- Fever (core body temperature >38.5°C)
- Marked leucocytosis (leucocyte count >15 × 10³/L)
- Rise in serum creatinine >50% above baseline
- Additional supporting factors: distension of large intestine, peri-colonic fat stranding, or colonic wall thickening at imaging

**Severe-complicated CDI (fulminant CDI):** One or more of the following attributed to CDI:
- Hypotension
- Septic shock
- Elevated serum lactate
- Ileus
- Toxic megacolon
- Bowel perforation
- Rapid deterioration of the patient

**Refractory CDI:** No response after 3–5 days of recommended CDI antibiotic treatment.

**Recurrence:** CDI recurs within 8 weeks after a previous episode resolved following completion of initial treatment.

## Treatment Algorithm (Fig. 1)

### Standard of Care (SoC) — Initial CDI

| Priority | Agent |
|----------|-------|
| 1st | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days |
| 2nd | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days |
| If preferred not available | Metronidazole 500 mg TID × 10 days |

### High Risk of Recurrence — Initial CDI

Risk factors for recurrence: age >65–70 years, healthcare-associated CDI, prior hospitalization in last 3 months, concomitant non-CDI antibiotic use, PPIs started during/after CDI diagnosis, prior CDI episode.

| Priority | Agent |
|----------|-------|
| 1st | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days |
| 2nd | SoC + Bezlotoxumab *(now discontinued — not available at SJN)* |

### 1st Recurrence

| Priority | Agent |
|----------|-------|
| 1st | SoC + Bezlotoxumab *(discontinued)* |
| 2nd | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days |
| If preferred not available | Vancomycin taper and pulse† |

### ≥2nd Recurrence

| Priority | Agent |
|----------|-------|
| 1st | FMT |
| 2nd | SoC + Bezlotoxumab *(discontinued)* |
| If preferred not available | Vancomycin taper and pulse† |

†Vancomycin taper and pulse: 125 mg QID × 2 weeks, then 125 mg BID × 1 week, then 125 mg daily × 1 week, then 125 mg every second day × 1 week, then 125 mg Q72H × 1 week.

### Severe CDI

Vancomycin or Fidaxomicin (no data supporting superiority of one over the other).
- When oral administration is not possible: intraluminal delivery (gastroduodenal or coloscopic) of vancomycin or fidaxomicin
- Do NOT routinely add IV metronidazole to oral SoC therapy for severe CDI

### Severe-Complicated CDI & Refractory Severe CDI

- Vancomycin or Fidaxomicin
- Multidisciplinary approach with **surgical consultation** (good practice statement)
- Consider IV tigecycline 50 mg BID (100 mg loading dose) on a case-by-case basis if deteriorating or progressing
- Total abdominal colectomy might be prevented by partial colectomy or loop ileostomy
- FMT may be a rescue therapy for severe-complicated CDI that has deteriorated despite CDI antibiotics and for whom surgery is not feasible

### When No Oral Treatment Is Possible

- Intraluminal (gastroduodenal or coloscopic) delivery of vancomycin or fidaxomicin
- Consider adjunctive IV metronidazole 500 mg TID or IV tigecycline 50 mg BID (100 mg loading dose)

## Detailed Recommendations by Question

### I. Initial CDI

- **Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days (Strong recommendation, Moderate level of evidence)**
- Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days is a strong alternative (Strong, High)
- Oral metronidazole 500 mg TID × 10 days should be used only when vancomycin and fidaxomicin are not available or feasible (Strong, Moderate)
- Vancomycin 500 mg QID not recommended (Strong, Very Low)
- Consider extended fidaxomicin (200 mg BID days 1–5, then 200 mg once daily on alternate days 7–25) for CDI episodes with increased risk of recurrence, especially elderly hospitalized patients (Weak, Low)
- Consider bezlotoxumab added to SoC for initial CDI with increased risk of recurrence if fidaxomicin not available *(now discontinued)*
- In non-severe CDI: may be acceptable to discontinue the inciting antibiotic and observe for 48 hours before starting CDI treatment (only in non-epidemic, non-hypervirulent strain setting)

### II. Severe and Severe-Complicated CDI

- Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days or Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days (Good practice statement)
- No data supporting superiority of one over the other for severe CDI
- For severe CDI: routine addition of IV metronidazole to oral SoC is NOT recommended (Weak, Very Low)
- Consider IV tigecycline on case-by-case basis if deteriorating to severe-complicated while on anti-CDI therapy

### III. When No Oral Treatment Is Possible

- Attempt intraluminal (gastroduodenal or coloscopic) delivery of vancomycin or fidaxomicin (Good practice statement)
- Consider adjunctive IV metronidazole 500 mg TID or IV tigecycline 50 mg BID (Weak, Very Low)

### IV. Refractory CDI

- For non-complicated refractory CDI not deteriorating: re-evaluate diagnosis; reconsider *C. difficile* colonization with diarrhea from another cause; consider non-adherence in outpatients
- For severe-complicated refractory CDI: multidisciplinary approach; surgical opinion sought early; FMT as rescue therapy (case-by-case basis)

### V. Recurrent CDI

- If initial episode treated with **vancomycin or metronidazole**: fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days is preferred for first recurrence (Strong, Low)
- If initial episode treated with **fidaxomicin**: consider addition of bezlotoxumab to SoC *(discontinued)* or fidaxomicin standard course
- Consider vancomycin tapering and pulse when fidaxomicin and bezlotoxumab are not available or feasible (Weak, Very Low)

### VI. Multiple Recurrent CDI

- FMT after SoC antibiotic pre-treatment or bezlotoxumab in addition to SoC (Weak, Moderate for FMT / Low for bezlotoxumab)
- Prospective data on second/further recurrences are limited

### VII. Prognostic Factors for Severe CDI

- **Older age (>65 years) and presence of multiple comorbidities are the most important risk factors for severe CDI (Strong, Moderate)**

### VIII. Prognostic Factors for Recurrent CDI

- **Older age (>65 years) — most important risk factor (Strong, Moderate)**
- Prior CDI episode(s) (Strong, Moderate)
- Healthcare-associated CDI and prior hospitalization in last 3 months (Weak, Low)
- Concomitant non-CDI antibiotic use during the diagnosis period (Weak, Very Low)
- PPIs started during/after CDI diagnosis (Weak, Very Low)

### IX. Prophylaxis

- Routine administration of probiotics to prevent CDI when on antibiotic treatment: **NOT recommended (Strong, Low)**
- Routine prophylaxis with anti-CDI antibiotics when on systemic antibiotic treatment: **NOT recommended (Good practice statement)**
- In very selected patients with history of multiple recurrent CDI precipitated by systemic antibiotic use: prophylaxis with microbiota-sparing anti-CDI antibiotics may be warranted after consultation with ID or Clinical Microbiology specialist (Good practice statement)

## General Measures (All CDI)

- Discontinuation of unnecessary antimicrobial therapy
- Adequate replacement of fluid and electrolytes
- Avoidance of anti-motility medications
- Review proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use
