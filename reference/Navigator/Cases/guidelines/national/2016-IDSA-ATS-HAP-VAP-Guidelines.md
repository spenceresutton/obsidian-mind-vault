# 2016 IDSA/ATS HAP/VAP Guidelines

## Overview

Published 2016 in *Clinical Infectious Diseases* (Kalil et al.). Major changes from 2005:
- GRADE methodology adopted throughout
- **HCAP (Healthcare-Associated Pneumonia) designation eliminated** — evidence did not support treating HCAP as HAP-equivalent
- Emphasis on using local antibiograms to drive empiric selection
- Biomarker-based guidance added (PCT, sTREM-1, CRP, CPIS)

Scope: non-immunocompromised adults with HAP (hospital-acquired, not on ventilator) or VAP (ventilator-associated).

---

## Microbiologic Diagnosis

**VAP (Rec. I):** Noninvasive semiquantitative cultures preferred over invasive quantitative sampling (BAL, PSB). No significant differences in 28-day mortality, overall mortality, or ICU LOS between approaches.

**HAP (Rec. III):** Obtain noninvasive respiratory samples before starting antibiotics to enable culture-guided therapy and de-escalation.

**Below-threshold invasive cultures (Rec. II):** PSB <10³ CFU/mL or BAL <10⁴ CFU/mL — withhold antibiotics, weigh clinical context.

---

## Biomarkers — What NOT to Use

| Marker | Recommendation | Rationale |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Procalcitonin (PCT) | **Strong AGAINST** adding to clinical criteria | 67% sensitivity, 83% specificity — insufficient to change management |
| sTREM-1 (BAL) | **Strong AGAINST** | Insufficient discriminatory power |
| CRP | **Weak AGAINST** | Insufficient discriminatory power |
| CPIS score | **Against** for diagnosis or discontinuation | Adds nothing to clinical assessment |

---

## VAP Empiric Treatment (Rec. X)

**Always cover:** *S. aureus*, *P. aeruginosa*, gram-negative bacilli.

**MRSA coverage — add when:**
- Patient has MRSA risk factors (Table 2 of guideline)
- Unit MRSA prevalence >10–20%
- Preferred agents: vancomycin or linezolid

**MSSA coverage (no MRSA risk):** piperacillin-tazobactam, cefepime, levofloxacin, imipenem, or meropenem

**Dual antipseudomonal agents — add when:**
- MDR *Pseudomonas* risk factors present, OR
- Unit resistance to monotherapy >10%

**Avoid:** aminoglycosides or colistin as monotherapy for VAP

---

## HAP Empiric Treatment (Rec. XII)

**S. aureus coverage required (strong):**
- MRSA: risk factors present, unit prevalence >20%, or high mortality risk → vancomycin or linezolid
- MSSA: no risk factors → piperacillin-tazobactam, cefepime, levofloxacin, imipenem/meropenem

**Gram-negative coverage required (strong):**
- Low-risk + local resistance ≤10%: single agent acceptable
- High-risk or local resistance >10%: dual agents

**Avoid aminoglycosides as sole antipseudomonal therapy**

---

## MDR Risk Factors (VAP)

Meta-analysis identified:
- Prior IV antibiotic use within 90 days
- ≥5 days hospitalization prior to VAP onset
- Septic shock at VAP onset
- ARDS before VAP
- Acute renal replacement therapy

---

## Pathogen-Specific Therapy

**MRSA HAP/VAP (Rec. XV):** Vancomycin or linezolid. Choose based on renal function, drug interactions, cost.

***P. aeruginosa* HAP/VAP (Recs. XVI–XVII):**
- Base definitive therapy on susceptibility testing
- Avoid aminoglycoside monotherapy
- Monotherapy acceptable without septic shock
- Combination therapy suggested for septic shock (mortality risk >25%)

**ESBL gram-negatives (Rec. XVIII):** Definitive therapy guided by susceptibility + patient factors.

***Acinetobacter* (Rec. XIX):**
- Prefer carbapenems or ampicillin/sulbactam if susceptible
- Colistin-susceptible only: IV polymyxin ± inhaled colistin
- **Recommend AGAINST** tigecycline
- **Recommend AGAINST** adjunctive rifampicin with colistin

**Carbapenem-resistant organisms (Rec. XX):** IV polymyxins (colistin/polymyxin B); consider adjunctive inhaled colistin.

**Inhaled antibiotics (Rec. XIV):** Add to systemic therapy for gram-negatives susceptible only to aminoglycosides or polymyxins.

---

## Duration & De-escalation

| Recommendation | Guidance |
|----------------|---------|
| VAP duration (Rec. XXI) | **7 days** — strong recommendation, moderate evidence |
| HAP duration (Rec. XXII) | **7 days** — strong recommendation |
| De-escalation (Rec. XXIII) | Narrow from broad-spectrum to targeted regimen when cultures return |
| PCT-guided discontinuation (Rec. XXIV) | Weak recommendation to use PCT + clinical criteria; NOT CPIS |

---

## PK/PD Optimization (Rec. XIII)

Determine dosing using PK/PD data rather than standard prescribing information alone. Relevant for beta-lactam extended infusions, aminoglycoside high-dose once-daily, etc.

---

## Key Teaching Points for Case Design

- **HCAP is gone** — prior HCAP designation led to overuse of broad-spectrum therapy; cases should NOT use HCAP as a reason to escalate empiric coverage
- **Local antibiogram drives empiric selection** — a case without knowing local resistance rates cannot definitively determine optimal empiric therapy
- **7-day duration is strong evidence** — cases testing whether to extend beyond 7 days should conclude: no, de-escalate instead
- **Aminoglycoside monotherapy is wrong** — any case answer selecting aminoglycoside monotherapy for HAP/VAP is incorrect
- **Dual antipseudomonal ≠ routine** — requires either risk factors OR documented local resistance >10%
