# 2021 IDSA/SHEA Focused Update: Management of *Clostridioides difficile* Infection in Adults

**Published:** Clinical Infectious Diseases, 2021;73(5):e1029–e1044
**Scope:** Focused update addressing fidaxomicin, vancomycin, and bezlotoxumab. Does not replace the 2017 full guideline for non-addressed topics.

---

## Summary of Recommendations

### PICO 1: Initial CDI Episode — Fidaxomicin vs Vancomycin?

**Recommendation:** Suggest fidaxomicin rather than vancomycin (conditional recommendation, moderate certainty).

- Vancomycin remains an acceptable alternative
- Fidaxomicin increases sustained response (RR 1.16; 95% CI 1.09–1.24) without increasing adverse events
- Implementation depends on available resources

### PICO 2: Recurrent CDI — Fidaxomicin vs Vancomycin?

**Recommendation:** Suggest fidaxomicin (standard or extended-pulsed) rather than standard vancomycin (conditional recommendation, low certainty).

- Vancomycin in a tapered and pulsed regimen or standard course are acceptable alternatives for a first recurrence
- For multiple recurrences: vancomycin tapered/pulsed, vancomycin + rifaximin, and FMT are additional options

### PICO 3: Bezlotoxumab as Co-Intervention?

**Recommendation:** For patients with a recurrent CDI episode within the last 6 months, suggest bezlotoxumab as a co-intervention along with SOC antibiotics (conditional, very low certainty).

> ⚠️ **Bezlotoxumab (Zinplava®) was discontinued January 31, 2025 — this recommendation is no longer actionable at SJN. See SJN guidelines.**

## Treatment Table (Table 1)

| Clinical Presentation | Recommended Treatment | Strength/Quality |
|----------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Initial CDI episode | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days (preferred) | Conditional/Moderate |
| | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days (alternative) | — |
| | Metronidazole 500 mg TID × 10–14 days (alternative for nonsevere CDI if above unavailable) | — |
| First CDI recurrence | Fidaxomicin 200 mg BID × 10 days OR extended-pulsed: BID × 5 days then every other day × 20 days (preferred) | Conditional/Low |
| | Vancomycin tapered/pulsed or standard course (alternative) | — |
| | Bezlotoxumab 10 mg/kg IV once adjunctive (now discontinued) | Conditional/Very Low |
| Second or subsequent recurrence | Fidaxomicin standard or extended-pulsed | — |
| | Vancomycin tapered/pulsed | — |
| | Vancomycin 125 mg QID × 10 days → rifaximin 400 mg TID × 20 days | — |
| | FMT (after ≥2 appropriate antibiotic treatment attempts) | — |
| Fulminant CDI | Vancomycin 500 mg QID PO or NG. If ileus: add rectal vancomycin instillation. IV metronidazole 500 mg q8h if ileus present | Strong/Moderate (oral VAN) |

**Nonsevere CDI definition:** WBC ≤15,000 cells/µL AND serum creatinine <1.5 mg/dL
**Fulminant CDI definition:** Hypotension or shock, ileus, megacolon

## Key Evidence Highlights

- Fidaxomicin vs vancomycin (initial CDI): 4 RCTs (n=1673); RR for sustained response 1.16; CDI initial clinical cure equivalent (RR 1.00)
- Fidaxomicin vs vancomycin (recurrent CDI): 3 RCT subgroup analyses; RR for sustained response at 30 days 1.27; data graded low certainty
- Bezlotoxumab: 2 phase III RCTs (MODIFY I/II); CDI recurrence RR 0.62 after initial cure; CHF caution (higher heart failure events in bezlotoxumab arm: 12.7% vs 4.8%)
