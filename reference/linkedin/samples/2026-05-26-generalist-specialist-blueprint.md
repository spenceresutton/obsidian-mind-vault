# Generalist-Specialist Blueprint

## Post

Generalist pharmacists don't lose arguments about antibiotic stewardship because they're wrong. They lose because they don't have the primary literature to back up what they already know.

Convincing an attending who ordered the wrong antibiotic requires fluency in the specific studies that justify de-escalation for this presentation, this pathogen, this acuity level. That expertise lives with ID specialists. Most floors don't have one.

Navigator's framework lets specialists deposit that expertise at scale. The ID pharmacist builds the clinical reasoning blueprint: the literature, the logic, the argument they'd make if they were standing there. Generalists execute from it, borrowing the specialist's credibility.

The generalist still makes the call. But they walk into the room backed by the specialist's literature.

## Angle

The barrier to antibiotic de-escalation isn't clinical recognition — it's the primary literature credibility required to advocate for the right answer. [[Navigator Product]] lets ID specialists encode that credibility into a reusable framework generalists can execute.
